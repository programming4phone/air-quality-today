Ozone

Ground level ozone occurs when sunlight reacts with nitrous oxide and volatile organic compounds. Industrial emissions, automobile exhaust, gasoline vapors, and chemical solvents are major sources of these substances. Breathing ozone creates health problems including chest pain, coughing, and throat irritation. It worsens bronchitis, emphysema, and asthma. Repeated exposure may permanently scar lung tissue.

PM2.5

Particulate matter is a mixture of extremely small particles and liquid droplets. Particles contain dust, metals, organic matter, and acids. Particles less than 10 micrometers in diameter can cause heart and lung problems. Inhalable coarse particles, found in dust and industrial waste, are between 10 and 2.5 micrometers in diameter. Fine particles (PM2.5), found in smoke and haze, are 2.5 micrometers, or less, in diameter.

Air Quality Index (AQI)

State and local environmental agencies publish air quality readings based on measurements taken by monitors located all over the United States. The EPA publishes this information through a web service called Air Now, which is used by this application.

Measurements are expressed in terms of numbers and color codes known as the Air Quality Index, described below:

Good
0-50
Air quality is considered satisfactory. Air pollution poses little or no risk.
Moderate
51-100
Air quality is acceptable. Moderate health concerns for people who are unusually sensitive to air pollution.
Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups
101-150
Members of sensitive groups may experience health effects. The general public is not likely to be affected. Sensitive groups include active adults, people with heart or lung disease (including asthma), older adults and children.
Unhealthy
151-200
Everyone may begin to experience health effects. Members of sensitive groups may experience more serious health effects.
Very Unhealthy
201-300
Health alert. Everyone may experience more serious health effects.
Hazardous
301-500
Emergency health warning conditions. The entire population is likely to be affected.
